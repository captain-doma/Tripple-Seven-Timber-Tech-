Preconstruction

At Tripple Seven Timber Tech, the Preconstruction Phase is all about laying a solid foundation for project success. Our team collaborates closely with clients, architects, and consultants to manage budgets, evaluate construction methods, and assess material availability from the outset. By staying actively involved during this phase, we ensure that every decision supports the project's objectives and long-term value. We also proactively manage timelines, ensuring that all construction documents are delivered on schedule. Through a transparent reporting system, we provide ongoing updates on costs, scheduling, and design coordinationâ€”helping clients make informed, timely decisions.

Site Evaluation

  • Review boundary and title documentation, and verify on-site property lines
  • Assess geotechnical reports and flag hidden cost factors
  • Confirm availability and proximity of utility connections (water, sewer, electricity)
  • Identify flood risks and drainage issues
  • Verify zoning regulations and environmental limitations
  • Flag off-site infrastructure upgrades required for the project

Cost Management & Estimating

  • Deliver value-engineering alternatives to optimize cost without compromising quality
  • Recommend practical material and construction system substitutions
  • Develop preliminary feasibility budgets
  • Identify bonding or guarantee requirements
  • Evaluate life-cycle costs for long-term savings
  • Provide initial cash flow projections and cost checkpoints

Design Development Phase

  • Coordinate closely with design professionals and the client team
  • Review plans for constructability and regulatory compliance
  • Recommend cost-saving solutions using practical and locally available materials
  • Resolve spatial and design conflicts through early coordination
  • Provide visual aids to help stakeholders and site crews understand the scope of work
  • Assist with sequencing plans and installation timelines to reduce on-site delays
  • Minimize the need for RFIs through clear documentation and proactive communication

Time Management & Scheduling

  • Establish a realistic milestone schedule
  • Identify approval and permitting requirements early on
  • Develop a phasing plan aligned with construction logistics
  • Build a master project schedule tailored to delivery goals
  • Identify critical long-lead materials and client-provided items for advanced coordination

Construction Document Phase

  • Assign experienced field personnel to manage on-site execution
  • Set up document tracking systems for submittals and approvals
  • Continue to review plans to catch potential discrepancies
  • Prepare and implement a quality control plan in partnership with the architect and client
